Here is a bias worth keeping. Pessimism sounds smart. Optimism sounds naive. But optimism is the only worldview that leads to action.

If you believe the future is bleak no matter what, there's no reason to try. If you believe the future can be better, you have every reason to work toward it. Optimism isn't about ignoring problems — it's about believing they can be solved.

## Optimism as a strategy

The most effective people are almost always optimists. Not because they're blind to reality, but because their optimism gives them the energy and persistence to keep working through difficulties.

Pessimists are often right in the short term. Things do go wrong. Plans fail. Systems break. But optimists are right in the long term, because they keep building despite the setbacks.

## The cost of cynicism

Cynicism is comfortable. It asks nothing of you. When something fails, the cynic gets to say "I told you so." But the cynic never built anything.

Every meaningful achievement in human history was accomplished by someone who believed it was possible. The cynics were always there, explaining why it couldn't be done. History doesn't remember them.

## Choosing your lens

You can look at the same set of facts and come to very different conclusions depending on your lens. The lens of pessimism sees decline everywhere. The lens of optimism sees opportunity.

Neither lens is more "realistic." They're both interpretive frameworks. The question is: which one leads to better outcomes?

Choose optimism. Not because it's easy, but because it works. This is why [quality software deserves your money](/quality-software-deserves-your-money) — the people who build it are optimists betting on a better future.
